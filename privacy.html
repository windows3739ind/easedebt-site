Privacy Policy

Thank you for using EaseDebt. Your privacy is important to us. This Privacy Policy explains what information EaseDebt collects, how it is used, stored and protected.

1. Information We Collect

  • Email address used for account creation and sign in.
  • Debt records, repayment history and notes you choose to enter.
  • Technical information required for authentication and cloud synchronization.
EaseDebt does not collect or store your banking passwords, debit card numbers, credit card numbers, UPI PINs or other payment credentials.

2. How We Use Your Information

  • Create and manage your account.
  • Synchronize your data across devices.
  • Calculate repayment progress and insights.
  • Improve reliability and respond to support requests.

3. Cloud Storage

Your information is securely stored using trusted cloud infrastructure powered by Supabase.

4. Data Security

We use industry-standard security measures and encrypted connections. While no online service can guarantee absolute security, we continuously work to protect your data.

5. Data Sharing

EaseDebt does not sell your personal information or share your financial records with advertisers. Information may only be shared when required by law or with trusted service providers necessary to operate the app.

6. Advertising

EaseDebt is completely ad-free. We do not display advertisements and do not use advertising identifiers for personalized advertising.

7. Google Play Purchases

If you purchase EaseDebt Pro, payments are processed securely by Google Play. EaseDebt does not collect or store your payment card information.

8. Your Data

You may update your information, delete debts and payments, or permanently delete your account and associated cloud data directly from within the app.

9. Children's Privacy

EaseDebt is not intended for children under the age required by local law.

10. Changes to this Policy

This policy may be updated from time to time. Material changes will be reflected on this page and within the app where appropriate.
